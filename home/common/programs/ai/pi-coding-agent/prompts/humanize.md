Review the following text and rewrite it to sound more natural and human.

**AI writing patterns to eliminate** (from Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing"):

1. **Filler phrases**: "It's important to note", "In today's world", "When it comes to"
2. **Excessive hedging**: "may potentially", "it could be argued", "in some cases"
3. **Redundant intensifiers**: "very unique", "extremely essential", "highly significant"
4. **Corporate buzzwords**: "leverage", "synergy", "robust", "comprehensive", "streamline"
5. **Predictable transitions**: "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally" at every paragraph
6. **Sycophantic openers**: "Great question!", "That's a fantastic point!"
7. **Cliché metaphors**: "tapestry of", "navigate the landscape", "at the heart of"
8. **Formulaic structures**: "X is not just Y, it's Z", "From A to B, C is..."
9. **Passive voice overuse**: Prefer active voice where natural
10. **List exhaustion**: Don't enumerate every possible case

**Goals:**
- Sound like a person wrote it, not a language model
- Vary sentence length and structure
- Use concrete examples over abstract statements
- Cut unnecessary words ruthlessly
- Keep the original meaning intact
